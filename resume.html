Recent Professional Experience:

BYU University Organizations (August 2019 - Present):

As a music major, my involvement in BYU started by performing as a clarinetist in every ensemble imagineable. Performing ensembles include the Marching Band, Wind Symphony, Philharmonic Orchestra, Group for New Music, and various chamber ensembles such as a woodwind quintet. As a composer, I have helped organize numerous composition recitals within the school of music as well as collaborated with many musicians ranging from soloists to the Philharmonic Orchestra. My desire to explore administrative oppurtunities within music led me to join the Student Advisory Council in 2023, where I coordinate events such as recitals and advocate for student needs within the school. Upon adding a Computer Science minor in Summer of 2023, I joined the BYU Developer's Club, where we study Website and App development while networking with industry professionals and working on personal and group projects.

BYU School of Music Teacher's Assistant (July 2022 - Present):

My TA work spans multiple classes and roles- the first of which was at the Music Tech Lab, where I helped students use and learn music software including Finale, Sibelius, Logic Pro, and Pro Tools. As a TA for classes such as Music Civilization, Theory, Form and Analysis, and Orchestration, I frequently tutored students, graded essays and assignments, and helped with class administration and organization of events such as music reading sessions.

Freelance Composer and Copyist (January 2018 - Present):

Collaborations with musicians involving commissions have led to performances in a variety of venues, including the International Clarinet Festival's ClarinetFest, the Navy Band Saxophone Symposium, and the North American Saxophone Alliance. Educational oppurtunities have led to performances at the Georgia Music Educator's Association's Annual Conference, June in Buffalo New Music Festival, and the Brevard Music Center. While at BYU, I have worked as a copyist for numerous professors as well as groups and musicians outside of BYU.

Custodian (January 2020 - August 2023):

While not normally featured on resumes, this job contains a one of the feats I am most proud of in my professional career. I worked the morning shift, which started at 4am or 5am each weekday. I worked on and off between 2020 and 2023 for about a year and a half in total, and during then, I never once slept through or missed a shift. While I have called in sick before (always at least a day in advance), I did take pride in showing up to every shift. I care about every job I perform regardless of glamour and will be the very best I can be in any situation.
