Upcoming Projects

Airship: for Woodwind Quintet (pre. April 2024)

'Airship' is a 5 minute concert opener for Woodwind Quintet that is inspired from the unique role that the French Horn plays within the quintet. Many composers approach the instrument differently, often requiring the horn to play quietly. I wanted to give the horn an oppurtunity to do the opposite. I think of the wind quintet as a texture often resembling the sky- nimble and expansive. I thought of the French Horn as a sort of airship, flying through an atmospheric texture created by the other instruments. 'Airship' is dynamic, expressive, and an exciting way to open a recital!

A Garden: Song cycle for Soprano, Cello, and Piano (prem. April 2024)

This song cycle is the result of a collaboration between myself and a poet, Alanna Hess. The poem uses a garden as a metaphor for sharing thoughts with the world- it's a journey from negative self talk and comparison to acceptance and humility. The cycle is set in 5 movements: 3 songs with an instrumental interlude between each.

Balm of Gilead: A Musical Essay on Sorrow and Joy

'Balm of Gilead', a long awaited projet of mine, is the culminating composition of my undergraduate music composition career. The work is anticipated to be between 10-15 minutes in length and dwells on many of my core principles as it relates to music. I write to find joy in all of the world around us. I find that to be impossible without acknowledging the darker moments of our existence- as they are what shape us and allow us to feel joy. I write to point people to that joy- not by pretending there are no difficulties in life, but by finding beauty in them. The term 'Balm of Gilead' was used in the Old Testament as a metaphor for relief from suffering. This piece depicts that relief- a relief that comes gradually through persistence and determination, as we often experience in our lives.
