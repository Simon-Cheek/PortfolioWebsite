Senior Recital - Program

Airship (Woodwind Quintet) - 5 Minutes

Airship is a concert opener dedicated to the excitement and the variety that comes with a woodwind quintet. The piece features the french horn in particular, giving the horn a chance to shine (and sometimes quite loudly). Aleatoric elements combined with thick textures makes for a foggy yet dynamic opener to the recital!


Pressed (Solo Viola) - 3 Minutes

Pressed is a work for solo viola that stands as an expression of desperation. The piece is full of turmoil and anxiety as the viola's sound takes on a more ferocious tone than usual. A somber yet strained middle section hints at a pained personality seeking relief desperately.


Dusk Descending upon Glass (Solo Violin) - 5 Minutes

Dusk Descending upon Glass is a playful solo work inspired by the Bonneville Salt Flats. In the right conditions, shallow water makes for a perfectly reflected ground that mirrors the sky, giving the illusion of walking on clouds. When combined with the flat terrain for miles, a scene full of nothing by sky and high reaching mountains creates an elegance that is nearly unmatched in the world. I imagine someone alone on the flats, seemingly dancing on the sky as the sun slowly begins to set. A hollow-ness to the piece suggets a scene with nothing for miles, but also a jubilance that matches the noon-day slowly approaching the nightfall.


Pulsating (Eb Clarinet and Contrabassoon) - 6 Minutes

Movement II

Pulsating is a dynamic yet eerie work featuring a rather odd combination of instruments. The mix of a toy-sounding Eb Clarinet with an instrument often compared to a foghorn undoubtedly breeds some humor. The light-hearted temper of the piece fades rather quickly into a more somber section, one full of repeated rhythms. The constant pulse of notes suggests a faint flicker of lights in an otherwise dark and dreary night.


Sonata of the Heights (Flute and Piano) - 12 Minutes

Movements I (Ascent), II (Summit), and III (Descent)

The excitement of this piece dates back to my sophomore year, when the mountains were newer to me. I loved watching the mountains dominate the horizon with a red-filled glow in the sunset and majestic snow-filled peaks in the Winter. Each movement of the sonata depicts a verious stage of a hiking trip through the mountains. The flute is bright, excited, and joyful and the piano is strong, firm, and passive. The second movement features a more intimate moment with nature, featuring extended solos for each individual instrument.


A Garden - Song Cycle for Soprano, Cello, and Piano (18 Minutes)


I. Bury Them

II. Interlude

III. Winter

IV. Ponderings

V. Even if They Don't Grow


Perhaps my most mature piece to date, A Garden stems from a collaboration with a friend of mine, Alanna Hess. Her text, shown below, follows 3 different poems in an journey of self-realization and confidence. A garden is used as a metaphor for self expression and ideas, one that the narrator struggles to express. Through episodes of self doubt, pity, and even anger comes a humility and confidence expressed through the final words of the cycle: "Dare to let my thoughts sprinkle out into the world, even if they don't grow." Each song is seperated by instrumental interludes, one for cello and piano, and one for solo cello.


Text by Alanna Hess:

I.

You speak words in rows
pastel flowers, pink and cream,
clip them for strangers to smell
and feel your feelings on their cheeks

I grow buds
that never blossom
thorns I cannot trim away
pricking blood
I do not plant those seeds again

Bury, bury, bury them

II.

You say cultivation
just takes a little education,

but where did you learn
to plant words people want to
pick up and smell and touch and listen
and listen
and listen
to you?

Winter freezes every growth
my thoughts wilt
feelings wither

and no one visits an empty garden

III.

Once I wove an entire lily
of words
you took it home in a purple vase
its fragrance a lucky haze
fading over days

I never grew another
kept all these seeds inside me
to die in the darkness

I’m tangled roots and dried stems
put me back together if you can
show me what it means to try again

Dare to let my thoughts sprinkle out
into the world
even if they don’t grow
